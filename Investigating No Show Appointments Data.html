Introduction¶

Patients not showing up for medical appointments can be a serious strain on a Country's health service. Being able to predict which patients are less likely to show up for such appointments can help plan around such contingencies.

For this particular investigation we will be using a dataset of 100k medical appointments in Brazil from May 2016 sourced from Kaggle, an online Data Science community.

We will be asking the following questions:

  • 1) Does Age Have A Noticable Influence On Paitents Who Miss Their Appointments
  • 2) What % Overall Of People Attended Their Appointment
  • 3) Are Patients On Scholarship Less Likely To Miss An Appointment?
  • 4) Do Medical Conditions Affect Attendance?
  • 5) Does Sending a SMS Alert Improve Attendance?

Conclusion

From here we can see that the average person who misses an appointment is likely to be younger than those who will make the appointment. We see that the average age of those who missed their appointment was 34.3 years old and the average age for those who did attend their appointment was 37.8 years old.

I personally do not believe this is a significant enough pattern however to really help doctor officers to plan around predicted missed appointments, at least not with the data we currently have.

In this case it might be useful to have more data as with 100K we are only looking 0.0005% of the population. Although I believe we would also need more tools and a better understanding of statistics than we have learnt so far in this module to better look at the data.

Conclusions¶

Summary

Our sample size included 100,000 different patients which I believe is a suitable amount of data for an analysis of this type as it allows us to look at the data as a whole (e.g. looking at the mean values) and be sure that the data is realtively accurate.
